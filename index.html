Project Overview

Here I will present three artifacts, three projects I have worked on in my final year at Southern New Hampshire University. One project is from a course final, the other two I worked on in my spare time in order to prep for an interview. An informal code review is also included.

Professional Self Assessment

Firstly, thank you so much for taking the time to view and examine my ePortfolio. This project will provide a look at my work as well as how I operate and how I think as an engineer. Starting my journey as a Computer Science student I had little to no experience programming. What I had completed could not be considered professional in any aspect up until that point.

Within this ePortfolio are three artifacts, each separate projects I have worked on during my senior year at SNHU. I am using three separate projects as I feel they demonstrate very specific skills each on their own.

My first artifact is my Computer Graphics and Visualization Final Project, where I demonstrate my skills in 3D modeling, C++ programming, as well as my ability to think as an engineer collaborating in a team environment as well as with stakeholders if necessary.

My second artifact is a Collaborative Editor, a very much simplified version of Google Docs. With this project my skills and know-how are on full display on the topic of data structures and algorithms, C# programming, and here specifically, my knowledge and skill utilizing Web Sockets.

My final artifact is a Notes application. This project was also made using C# while also utilizing MSSQL. Because of its nature and quality, I decided this project would be a great addition to my ePortfolio because of its demonstration of my knowledge and skill with programming in C# as well as setting up and properly manipulating databases.

Through all of these projects, and especially with their enhancements made in my final term at SNHU, I made sure to look at my own past projects empirically and with a security mindset. I made sure to properly examine and test my code so that security flaws and vulnerabilities were properly tested and weeded out.

Though it is not present within this ePortfolio for contractual reasons, I am currently working as a Full Stack Software Engineer on a Patient Information Management System as well as a smaller feature, an Audio-to-Text Transcribing application. My time at both Sierra College and Southern New Hampshire University has prepared me to tackle this project professionally. While this project is ongoing I have made sure to communicate properly with my employer as well as document my work thoroughly through in-code comments as well as by using project management software (Trello and Balsamiq) and creating easy to read and understand documentations in the form of Software Design documents.
